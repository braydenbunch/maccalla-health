Parts A & B

Original Medicare

The foundation of Medicare — but not the full picture. Parts A and B are managed by the federal government and cover a wide range of care, but not everything. Knowing what's included (and what isn't) is the key to smart decisions.

Part A

Hospital insurance

Part A helps cover care you receive while admitted to a hospital or skilled nursing facility, plus hospice and limited home health care. Most people don't pay a premium for Part A if they or their spouse paid Medicare taxes while working.

  • Inpatient hospital stays
  • Skilled nursing facility care (after a qualifying hospital stay)
  • Hospice services
  • Limited home health care

Coverage is broad, but there are still deductibles and coinsurance you're responsible for.

Part B

Medical insurance

Part B helps cover outpatient care — doctor visits, diagnostic testing, preventive screenings, and durable medical equipment. It comes with a monthly premium, which most people pay directly from their Social Security benefits.

  • Doctor visits and outpatient care
  • Preventive services (screenings, vaccines, wellness visits)
  • Lab work and diagnostic tests
  • X-rays, MRIs, CT scans
  • Medical equipment like walkers or oxygen tanks

After the annual deductible, Medicare pays 80% of approved costs — you're responsible for the remaining 20%.

The part that surprises people

What Original Medicare doesn't cover

While Parts A and B cover a lot, they don't cover everything — and there's no out-of-pocket maximum, which means costs can keep adding up with frequent or serious medical needs.

  • Prescription drugs (you'll need a separate Part D plan)
  • Dental, vision, or hearing care
  • Long-term or custodial care (nursing homes, assisted living)
  • Coverage while traveling outside the U.S.
  • Protection from high out-of-pocket costs

Because of these gaps, most people add a Medicare Supplement plan or enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan for more predictable, manageable costs.
