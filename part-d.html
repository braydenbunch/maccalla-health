Prescription drugs

Part D drug plans

Original Medicare doesn't cover most prescription drugs — that's where Part D comes in. These private plans work alongside your Medicare coverage to protect you from high drug costs.

How it works

Formularies, tiers, and pharmacies

Every Part D plan has a list of covered drugs — a formulary — divided into tiers, from low-cost generics to brand-name and specialty medications. What you pay depends on the plan, the tier, and whether your pharmacy is in-network.

You can get Part D coverage two ways: a standalone plan (often paired with Original Medicare or Medigap), or through a Medicare Advantage plan that includes drug coverage — most do.

Why it matters — even now

The penalty nobody warns you about

Even if you take no prescriptions today, enrolling when you're first eligible matters. Waiting can lead to late enrollment penalties that stick with you for life — and having coverage in place means you're protected the moment your health changes.

The same prescriptions can cost wildly different amounts on different plans. We'll compare your options based on:

  • Your current prescriptions
  • Your preferred pharmacies
  • Monthly premium vs. total yearly costs
  • Coverage gaps and out-of-pocket limits

Even with a solid drug plan, Medicare leaves behind deductibles, copays, and coinsurance with no limit. Supplement plans can cover those gaps.
