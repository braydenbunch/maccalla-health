Medigap

Medicare Supplement plans

Original Medicare leaves you exposed to deductibles, copays, and 20% coinsurance with no cap. Supplement plans — Medigap — are designed to pay for what Medicare doesn't, working alongside it, not replacing it.

How it works

Medicare pays first, Medigap covers the rest

With a Supplement plan, Medicare still pays its share of covered services — then your Medigap plan kicks in to cover all or part of what's left: deductibles, coinsurance, copays. Fewer surprise bills, more predictability.

And you keep the freedom to see any doctor or hospital that accepts Medicare. No networks, no referrals, no ZIP-code limitations.

Medigap plans are standardized by the federal government — Plan G from one company has the same core benefits as Plan G from another. The difference is price and customer service, not coverage. That's exactly where a broker earns their keep.

Plan comparisons

The plans people ask about most

Most comprehensive for new enrollees

Plan G

Covers everything Original Medicare doesn't — except the Part B deductible. Once that's met, nearly all costs are covered. The choice for maximum predictability.

  • Part A deductible & coinsurance
  • Part B coinsurance (the 20%)
  • Skilled nursing facility care
  • Foreign travel emergency (up to plan limits)
  • Part B excess charges
Lower premium, some cost sharing

Plan N

A solid option for lower monthly premiums. You pay small copays for some doctor and ER visits.

  • Part A deductible & coinsurance
  • Part B coinsurance (small copays on some visits)
  • Skilled nursing facility care
  • Foreign travel emergency

Doesn't cover: Part B deductible or excess charges

Basic core coverage

Plan A

The most minimal Medigap plan — some help, with higher out-of-pocket exposure.

  • Part A coinsurance and hospital costs
  • Part B coinsurance
  • Blood (first 3 pints)
  • Hospice care coinsurance

Doesn't cover: Part A/B deductibles, skilled nursing, foreign travel

Dental, vision, hearing, and serious-illness protection aren't included in Medicare or Medigap. That's where additional coverage rounds things out.
